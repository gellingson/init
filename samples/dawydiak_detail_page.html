1989 Benetton B189-01 Formula One Racing Car. The first of seven chassis constructed

Cosworth DFR 3.5L V8 normally aspirated engine built by Nicholson McLaren in Wokingham, England.

(Not DFS 2.65 as stamped on valve cover)

Stamped "DFR 3196" 

 
Designed by South African Rory Bryne.

 

This car was the earlier chassis to the 1989 Japanese Grand Prix winning car with Alessandro Nannini at the wheel. That race was profiled in the documentary 'Senna'. Nannini was the default winner after Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost collided.

 

This car has been driven to points paying finishes by Nannini; 3rd in San Marino and the British GP with it's best finish a 2nd at Australia.

 

This Benetton B189 was completely rebuilt from tub up to final successful testing at Silverstone by the finest of fabricators Liaz Jakhara of the renowned ZUL RACING COMPANY in Derby, England and has been driven only on test days at Sears Point/Infineon Raceway since delivery. The engine was rebuilt by Nicholson McLaren to a DFR 2.5 Liter Normally Aspitrated Spec.

Engine Value alone estimated at 100,000 GBP Sterling.

The remainder of the car is precisely as it ran in F1 Spec.  Including the gearbox.

 

Large spares package is included with this car as well as spare floor and wheels.

 

Original car cover, Original team clothing, (4) Original Goodyear racing tires, (4) New full wets Avon A15 984CLW.
