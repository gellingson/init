This 1988 Mercedes Benz 560SL is a two owner car purchased by the seller in Spring 2013. Described as being in excellent condition with sorted mechanicals, fully functional electrics and cold-blowing A/C, sale includes a well-preserved factory hardtop. The car is all factory correct down to the radio, and the seller notes that this example retains all original paint, is garage kept, rust-free and serviced regularly. It has just over 90k original miles.

Cosmetics remain nice with good paint, panel fit, brightwork and glass. We’re happy to see no dealer-applied fender trim, and the color-matched, factory-supplied hardtop is a big plus for year-round driving. Paint is thought to remain 100% original with no evidence of past touch-ups, spot resprays or other repair work. Garaged year round, plastics and rubber are holding up well with no signs of shrinking, drying or deterioration.

The cabin presents similarly well with an un-cracked dash top, fully functional gauges, switches and accessories including recently serviced, cold-blowing A/C. Seat cushions have recently been reupholstered in leather using the correct color and stitching, but uppers do display some minor cracking. Rear jump seats are original and in excellent shape, as is the hardtop’s headliner. Additionally, the power soft top is good working condition with a clear plastic rear windows and rip-free fabric.

Garage kept and regularly serviced, the car is said to start easily and run very well. Recent work includes the aforementioned A/C service, a transmission flush and fluid change, new tires and an alignment. The seller estimates to have more than $20,000 invested including initial purchase price and subsequent maintenance, and adds that it brakes, tracks and rides as nicely as it runs.

Premoto1

These are exceptionally well made cars and are very reliable. Repairs don’t have to be exorbitantly expensive if one stays on top of them and the car is maintained by a marque expert. The downside is that, because the cars are so well made, they stand up to deferred maintenance and repairs for a lomg time. This particular example looks like a clean example in a good color combination. Having said that, there are a few bits to pick. The radio (pictured) is not original. the softtop will not be power operated. My belief is the jump-seat was an aftermarket addition. I don’t be they were offered by Mercedes. A couple other items of note, the wiper blades are aftermarket (no big deal until you try to by the factory refillable units and find they’re $300 for the set). The expansion tank cap is a Pep Boys unit. The car has the incorrect type of coolant and I he brake fluid looks quite dark. None of these issues are particularly bad but indicate a lack of detail by the maintaining shop. Doing it right is usually no more difficult (and sometimes easier) than doing it wrong. Service history would be a big plus. Given the mileage the car will be due for a chain, tensioner, and rails. Also, it’s likely the engine and subframe mounts are worn and collapsed. This issue give these cars a very harsh, truck-like feel when going over rough pavement. Replacing them transforms ride quality. Overall, this looks like a good foundation for a classy Sunday driver. Take the top off, put it in the corner of the garage, and go enjoy some sun (in about three months, depending where you are).
